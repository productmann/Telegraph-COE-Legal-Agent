### **Point 4: Don't Flag Standard English Law Provisions**

**Feedback:** "No exclusion for death/personal injury liability" - this is standard under English law and cannot be excluded even if not explicitly stated. Do not flag this.

**Change Made:**
- ‚ùå OLD: "No exclusion for death/personal injury liability, though this is standard. Override terms ¬ß4.3 confirms this cannot be excluded."
- ‚úÖ NEW: Skip this entirely - do not flag provisions that are standard under English law

**Implementation:**
- Added specific example to Step 4:
  ```
  ‚ùå DO NOT say: "No exclusion for death/personal injury liability, though this is standard"
  ‚úÖ DO say: Nothing - English law already prohibits excluding death/personal injury 
              liability, so this is not a gap to flag
  ```
- Added to "Do NOT flag provisions that are:" list:
  - Standard under English law (e.g., inability to exclude death/personal injury liability)
  - Required by statute and therefore implied even if not stated
  - Standard practice that would apply regardless of contract language
- Removed example 3.2 from formatted example (vendor cannot exclude death/personal injury)
- Added to self-check validation
- Updated "Deprioritise/Skip" section

**Rationale:** Certain protections exist under English law regardless of contract language. Flagging their absence suggests a gap that doesn't actually exist, which could mislead business teams.

Using "clauses" for contract provisions follows standard English legal practice and matches how TMG's lawyers actually write.
